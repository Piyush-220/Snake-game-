Solar System

Solar System

Age: 4.6 billion years

Number of Planets: 8

Central Star: Sun

The Solar System is the gravitationally bound system of the Sun and the objects that orbit it. It formed approximately 4.6 billion years ago from the gravitational collapse of a giant interstellar molecular cloud.

History

The Solar System formed from a large rotating cloud of gas and dust known as the solar nebula. Over time, gravity pulled material together to form the Sun and the surrounding planets.

Structure

The Solar System consists of the Sun, eight planets, dwarf planets, moons, asteroids, and comets. The inner planets are rocky, while the outer planets are gas giants and ice giants.

Planets

The eight planets of the Solar System are Mercury, Venus, Earth, Mars, Jupiter, Saturn, Uranus, and Neptune.
